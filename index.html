HealthPilot has a funnel and no clinic. The White-Label has a clinic and no funnel at all. Each is the missing half of the other.
Where it stands today: nothing connects them. HealthPilot's "handoff" is a redirect — on its provider page it opens a confirm dialog and links the visitor out to the provider's website (provider-detail/page.tsx). A

Stay Separate

Two independent products that never talk to each other. The current reality, made deliberate.
● Build effort: none Fully reversible Zero coupling risk

When this is the right answer

When HealthPilot is either a genuine standalone consumer-wellness brand with its own audience, or it's being parked. In both cases you accept the two systems don't share patients. This is also the correct holding position while the White-Label gets to its first live clinic, since it costs nothing.

B

Bridge

Keep both products, connect them with a one-way handoff so HealthPilot's leads reach the clinic with their data attached.
● Build effort: moderate (weeks) Reversible (it's integration, not a merge) Keeps HP as its own brand

When this is the right answer

When HealthPilot stays a live, independently-branded funnel and the clinic is one destination it routes men's-health traffic to. This is the only shape that supports a genuine multi-provider marketplace (HealthPilot listing Ted's alongside other, even non-BGV, providers) while still getting qualified leads into the clinic.

▸ The deeper variant (B2 — full data handoff), step by step

  1. Anonymous HealthPilot user finishes intake + bloods; AI matches a men's-health pathway and they choose the clinic.
  2. HealthPilot fires the handoff — the genuinely new HP-side piece. It either pushes its dormant ProviderWebhook with a signed payload, or exposes a one-time GET /platform/export?token=… the clinic can pull from. Today HP has neither; its handoff only redirects.
  3. The clinic receives it at a new POST /platform/handoff that mirrors the existing Ryft webhook receiver exactly: public route, HMAC-verify the raw body, idempotency.recordIfNew, then act.
  4. The clinic creates the patient with existing primitives: createAuthUser() (Firebase) + a Patient row + setClinicClaims(uid, clinicId), and stamps attributionSource = 'healthpilot' (two new columns).
  5. Bloods land as BloodTest(sourceType = EXTERNAL) with ifLogicOutcome set — the verified gate that lets a clinician later skip CBT.
  6. Auto-login reuses WL's existing handoff token: mint a Firebase custom token (createCustomToken), redirect to /from/healthpilot on the clinic domain, exchange via signInWithCustomToken. WL already does precisely this for its internal clinic handoff.
  7. Optional reverse webhook: on first paid invoice, the clinic posts patient.converted back to HealthPilot, which writes an AttributionEvent for revenue tracking.
Two honest caveats.  Identity: HP uses JWT, WL uses Firebase — there's no shared session, so the bridge mints a fresh Firebase identity at handoff. The patient is still a separate record in WL, linked by attribution, not unified.  Commission: HealthPilot can't enforce it because no money flows through it; settlement is internal BGV rev-share, with the reverse webhook as the data source.

  • Preserves HealthPilot as its own brand and the multi-provider vision

  • Commission can't be enforced; relies on internal settlement + a reliable reverse webhook

C

Unify

Absorb HealthPilot's discovery brain into the White-Label. One platform, one patient identity, first click to lifetime care.
● Build effort: large (months) Every piece is permanent HP-the-product retired

When this is the right answer

When HealthPilot is really BGV's own funnel rather than a neutral marketplace, and you want to own the men's-health vertical end to end. The deciding test: does HealthPilot ever send patients to companies that aren't BGV's? If no, this is the destination.

🧩 How it works, fundamentally

HealthPilot's valuable mechanisms are rebuilt as new domains inside the White-Label monorepo (it's read-only, so "absorb" means reimplement the good parts, not merge repos). The funnel and the clinic become one continuous system.

  1. A cold visitor hits a clinic domain and does anonymous AI triage — a new WL module. No login required.
  2. Matching routes them to the right pathway (trt-diagnosis / ed-onboarding / a one-off product) and interprets any uploaded bloods up front.
  3. On "start treatment," WL converts the anonymous lead into a real patient using its existing signup primitives — the intake is carried over, no re-entry, same record.
  4. From there it's the existing machine: consult → prescribe → pharmacy → payment → lifetime management, all in one system under one clinic brand.
The genuinely hard part. The White-Label has no anonymous state today — Patient.uid is the Firebase uid, so a patient only exists after login. Unify needs a new anonymous "pre-patient" layer (a Lead / pre-intake record linked to the Patient on conversion), mirroring HealthPilot's /auth/anonymous + link-anonymous-intake. That's the most novel piece, plus a PHI-at-rest posture for pre-patient data.

Pros

  • One patient record, first click to lifetime care — no seam, ever
  • Demand feeds your own fulfilment → full treatment margin captured
  • WL is already multi-tenant, so every clinic gets the funnel for free
  • The funnel is finally men's-health-specific — no spillage
  • Strategically clean: no captive-marketplace conflict of interest

  • Requires the strategic commitment that HP is not a neutral marketplace

Where the analysis leans

Toward C · Unify — if the strategy supports it

All three are real, and A or B is genuinely correct under the right conditions. But for a business whose value lives in owning the men's-health patient relationship, one platform is the only end-state with no permanent seam and full margin capture. The lean is to C, with eyes open about what makes A or B right instead.

🎯 When each option actually wins

Holding position

The lean

The one decision that picks between B and C

Is HealthPilot ever meant to send patients to companies that aren't BGV's?

If NO → (the expected answer)
C · Unify. Absorb the acquisition engine into the White-Label and own the vertical end to end.
If YES → the only world where B/A is right
B · Bridge (or A). HealthPilot stays a distinct, neutral marketplace business, staffed and capitalised as its own company.
🧭

However we sequence it, we build for the permanent end-state. If the answer is C, we don't build B first as a throwaway bridge — we build the real, permanent pieces of C in a sound order. The right architecture, made to last, not a short-term fix that has to be ripped out later.
