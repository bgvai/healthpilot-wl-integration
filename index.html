01 · The finding

They aren't two versions of the same thing. They're the two halves of one company.

HealthPilot generates and qualifies demand. The White-Label fulfils it. There is almost no feature overlap — which is exactly why the question "integrate or separate" is worth answering properly rather than by reflex.

The committed answer

Long-term, these become one platform — permanently.

Not two services joined by a bridge. Not two services kept apart. HealthPilot's discovery and triage engine becomes the permanent top of the White-Label funnel, and HealthPilot as a standalone product stops existing. One patient, one record, one journey, white-labelled per clinic.

03 · The architectures considered

There are only three shapes this can take. Two of them are wrong.

Stating all three openly is the point — the verdict isn't a preference, it's what's left standing once the alternatives are followed to their consequences.

Rejected

04 · The reasoning

Four forces, all pointing the same way

Any one of these is a strong argument. Together they make permanent separation, or a permanent bridge, the wrong long-term bet.

1

The economics only point one way

Clinical fulfilment earns treatment margin — TRT is a recurring subscription with lifetime value measured in years. A referral marketplace earns thin, one-time commission.

The value in a men's-health business is in owning the patient relationship and the treatment, not in selling a click. Demand generation should feed your own fulfilment, never hand it onward.

A bridge that passes patients to a separate system leaks the single most valuable thing you have.
2

A captive marketplace is structurally fake

An independent marketplace only works if it neutrally lists many providers — including your competitors. But BGV owns the clinic, so the matcher will always favour it.

Competitors notice, and leave. You're left with a "marketplace" of one. The honest version of that product is simply "BGV's front door."

If it's really your own funnel, it belongs inside the platform — not standing beside it pretending to be neutral.
3

"Perfect long-term" means one record, no seam

The patient journey is one arc: anonymous first touch → triage → becoming a patient → years of treatment. The correct data architecture is a single identity across all of it.

Two services means a bridge forever: duplicated patients, ongoing PHI sync, a permanent reconciliation surface and a permanent place for things to break.

A seam you maintain forever is the definition of a thing that works short-term and rots. The end-state has no seam.
4

The generic-vs-specific gap resolves by specialising

HealthPilot is wellness-generic (gut, sleep, energy, weight). The White-Label is men's-health-specific (TRT + ED). Bridged forever, most of HealthPilot's funnel spills nowhere.

Absorbed, you keep the mechanism — anonymous AI intake, matching, blood interpretation — and aim all of it at men's health.

The funnel and the drain finally match. The wide net becomes a focused one.

06 · What carries forward

Keep HealthPilot's brain. Drop its marketplace.

Absorbing HealthPilot does not mean carrying all of it. The valuable part is the acquisition engine. The marketplace half is a different, weaker business that conflicts with the core — it does not come along.

✓ Keep — the acquisition engine

This is what the White-Label completely lacks today.
  • Anonymous AI intake / triageThe soft front door for cold traffic the clinic has never had
  • Matching & eligibility scoringRoutes a visitor to the right pathway automatically
  • Blood interpretationImported results can enable a clinician to skip CBT
  • Customisable intake-flow conceptPer-clinic questionnaires without rebuilding UI each time

✕ Drop — the marketplace half

A neutral-marketplace business that competes with your own clinic.
  • Listing third-party / competitor providersConflicts directly with owning the patient
  • Provider ratings & benchmarkingOnly matters when you're a neutral broker
  • Supplement affiliate linksThin commission, off-strategy for a clinic
  • Redirect-out handoffReplaced by in-platform conversion — no leak
The single decision that changes the destination

One question decides this — and it's yours, not the code's.

Is HealthPilot ever meant to send patients to companies that aren't BGV's?
