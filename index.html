Why it works today

  • The receiving side already exists in WL
  • Bloods can enter via sourceType = EXTERNAL → lets a clinician skip CBT (verified gate)
  • WL already has an inbound webhook + idempotency layer
  • No change to HealthPilot's product or UX — it stays as-is

The trade-off

  • Two brands, two databases, two logins forever
  • Patient still makes a 2nd account on WL (pre-filled, but separate)
  • HP can't collect commission — no money flows through it
  • Right shape only IF HP is a true marketplace of many providers
WL side (most of the work): one inbound POST /platform/handoff + a /from/healthpilot auto-login page + 2 attribution columns on the patient.
HP side (small): to carry the bloods (not just the user), HP exposes intake + bloods for WL to fetch via a one-time token. No UX/product change.

The upside

  • One brand, one journey, no double registration
  • Payments & attribution fully under WL's control
  • The smoothest possible patient experience

The trade-off

  • Much bigger build — you rebuild HP's good parts inside WL
  • You use ~30% of HP and drop the marketplace half (ratings, other providers, supplements)
  • It decides HP is not a marketplace — it's WL's marketing funnel
  • HP is wellness-generic; WL is men's-health — you narrow it on purpose
Because HP is read-only and Arief's team won't take big changes, "absorb" really means building a new discovery module inside WL — not merging the repos.

The one question that decides it

The code can't answer this — only you can. It changes everything downstream:

Is HealthPilot meant to be an independent marketplace listing many providers (incl. non-BGV)?
Or is it really just the top-of-funnel for BGV's own men's-health clinics?

If → independent marketplace
Go Option 1, and never absorb. Ted's is one provider among many; the bridge is the whole point.
If → really just BGV's funnel
Go Option 1 now as a stepping stone, but you're heading to Option 2 — build the bridge knowing that.

My recommendation

Start with Option 1 either way. It's small, low-risk, reversible, and respects "HP stays as-is." It would also be the first time a HealthPilot lead arrives at the clinic with their data attached — today HP just links the user out to a provider's website, carrying no intake or blood data, and its handoff/webhook code sits dormant. Begin the bridge; let the answer to the question above tell us whether it's the destination or a stepping stone.
